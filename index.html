The largest wind farm of India in Muppandal, Tamil Nadu. The development of wind power in India began in the 1990s, and has significantly increased in the last few years. Although a relative newcomer to the wind industry compared with Denmark or US, domestic policy support for wind power has led India to become the country with the fourth largest installed wind power capacity in the world. Wind power installations occupy only 2% of the wind farm area facilitating the rest of the area for agriculture, plantations, etc.


Renewable vs non-renewable energy resource

Natural resources can be grouped into two groups: renewable and non-renewable. Renewable resources will naturally replenish themselves over time, like wind, solar, plants, trees, etc. Non-renewable will be gone forever once used, like coal, fuel, etc. Renewable energy resource is the energy we derive from many of Earth's naturally occurring resources, including wind, water, and sun. We call these resources renewable or sustainable (as in sustainable energy) since they are inexhaustible. The rate at which we're using fossil fuels is too fast. If we don't make a drastic and immediate change in how we"re using them, they could be potentially depleted within our children's lifetime. On the other hand,renewable energy comes from virtually inexhaustible sources. These resources are called nonrenewable energy resource.

Opinions

Wind Energy: Advantages & Limitations

Advantages of wind energy

  1. Since it is produced domestically, it reduces imports from other nations.
  2. Wind turbines are installed in rural areas with large plain grounds, facilitating power availability in villages.
  3. It is inexhaustible and does not cause air pollution.
  4. It can work in the day, night, and cloudy weather.

Solar Energy: Advantages & Limitations

Advantages of solar energy

  1. Solar panels don't make noise.
  2. You can install the panels on rooftops and ground too.
  3. This source is more predictable.
  4. It requires little maintenance.

Limitations of solar energy

  1. This source is highly weather-dependent. It may have some problems operating at night and in rough weather.
  2. Though solar panels don't require much maintenance, they are fragile, which means you must take special care during installation.
  3. Selecting the right place for installation can be tricky.
  4. Storing the electricity in batteries is expensive.
